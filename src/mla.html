My A-Level Project

The Cars

Each car is made with a box that represents the car and five raycasts that act as the vision of a driver. The box of the car is then placed within an axis aligned bounding box to increase the efficiency of collision checks. Cars can have three colours: black, green and red. black cars are active, green are the best from the previous generation and red cars are inactive or dead.

The Track

Tracks are made up of lots of straight lines. Each line has an axis aligned bounding box surrounding it. Then each line is paired another line nearby and another box surrounds the two. This is repeated until there is a large box that contains the entire track. This makes it extremely fast for a car to check if it is colliding with the track as it can traverse down the binary tree only checking the boxes that the car is in.

The Architecture

The cars use a Deep Feedforward Neural Network. Each input neuron is a distance value from a raycast. That value is then normalised. It is then passed through three hidden layers. Finally, it passed to the output layer. One node controls the speed of the car and the other determines the steering angle.
